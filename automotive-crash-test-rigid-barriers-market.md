<p><p>Automotive crash test rigid barriers are essential in assessing vehicle safety during impact collisions. The frontal impact barrier simulates head-on crashes, evaluating a vehicle's structural integrity and occupant protection from direct frontal hits. In contrast, the side impact barrier tests a vehicle's resilience against lateral collisions, which are more common and can be particularly dangerous. Both barrier types provide crucial data to manufacturers and regulators to improve vehicle designs and enhance passenger safety in real-world scenarios.</p></p>
