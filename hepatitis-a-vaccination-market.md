<p><p>The Hepatitis A vaccination market is primarily divided into two types: inactivated vaccines and live attenuated vaccines. Inactivated vaccines contain virus particles that have been killed, prompting an immune response without causing disease. They are widely used due to their safety and efficacy. Live attenuated vaccines, on the other hand, utilize a weakened form of the virus, stimulating a robust immune response while being less common for Hepatitis A. Both types play crucial roles in preventing Hepatitis A infection globally.</p></p>
